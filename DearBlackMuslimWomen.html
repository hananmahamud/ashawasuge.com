When I was a little girl, I always dreamed of seeing someone who looked like me be represented in a good light. The closest admiration I had was Oprah and Michelle Obama. Their story and life mission are most admirable to me. But, I never came across a Black Muslim woman with a hijab reminding us to shoot for the stars. As I get older, I decided to become that representation for many black Muslim girls in my community. To remind them that they are beyond capable of greatness. To shoot for the stars. To remind them that they are loved and valued. To share stories on making spaces for each other. We are so beyond capable of so much. And I can’t wait to continue on this path of sharing our stories, our lived experiences, and most importantly our hearts.

“The most disrespected woman in America is the black woman. The most un-protected person in America is the black woman. The most neglected person in America, is the black woman” -Malcolm X

Imagine adding Muslim to that identity. You can try to break us down, but we will always rise. We will continue to break barriers and do everything with faith.

I love y’all. I support y’all. And I am on a mission to creating a table for y’all to sit at without any judgment or fear of not fitting in. In this space- you are free to be who you are and be whatever your heart desires.

Sisterly love,
A.W
